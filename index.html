Methodology▾
Scale
500+ clients, done personally
Every client in under 12 months — SME, reseller, and enterprise accounts simultaneously. At peak, managing multiple active onboardings across different stages: intake, setup, enablement, launch, and handover — all running in parallel.
Enterprise experience
Up to 10,000 employees
Largest client onboarded: ~10,000 employees. Delivered HR demonstrations, town halls, and end-user enablement sessions. Managed HR directors and C-suite stakeholders through the full implementation journey.
  • 1
    Structured intake — zero missing information

    Designed a conditional intake form in Monday.com that captures every required field based on client type. Auto-creates the board item. No email chains, no back-and-forth. Setup starts immediately.

  • 2
    Platform configuration

    Account creation, feature activation, branding, and admin access — configured in CMS and CRM using intake data. Consistent every time, regardless of client size.

  • 3
    Welcome comms — AI-generated, automatically delivered

    Client welcome pack generated by Claude from live board data — pre-filled, formatted, ready. Mailchimp handles timing. Zero manual copy-paste.

  • 4
    Enablement — demos, workshops, town halls

    10-minute intro or 45-minute full platform session — tailored to client size. Admin training, HR demonstrations, and end-user town halls designed to drive adoption and confidence from day one.

  • 5
    Launch + 2-week support window

    I remain primary contact post-launch. All queries via a structured request form — tracked on the board. Nothing lost in inboxes.

  • 6
    Structured handover

    Handover email auto-triggered. Feedback survey sent. Board closed. Account team has everything before I step back. No dependency on memory.

📊
The result: 83% reduction in admin time. The process runs itself. I focus on the client — not the admin.

AI Implementation Project
Company-wide AI tool rollout — stakeholders, cohorts, blockers, lessons
AI Project▾
🎯 Recruiter note — why this matters
This is the project that shows real PM capability — stakeholder mapping from scratch, phased rollout design, dependency management, cross-functional coordination, and an honest account of what went wrong and how it was fixed. This is not theoretical project management.
The Project
Company-wide AI clinical tool rollout
Coordinated the implementation of an AI documentation tool for healthcare professionals — deployed across the business in phased cohorts. The tool auto-generates consultation notes during client calls, freeing clinicians to focus on the person rather than manual note-taking.
My Role
Implementation Coordinator — end to end
New to the company when this landed. I built the stakeholder map from scratch, gathered requirements from 6+ teams, wrote the SOP, designed the rollout plan with milestones and dependencies, coordinated cohort delivery, and wrote the test cases for UAT myself.
  • 1
    Stakeholder mapping — built from zero

    No existing map. I worked with my director to identify every stakeholder across Clinical, Legal, Compliance, Marketing, Product, Customer Success, and the external vendor — then met each team individually to surface all requirements before planning began.

  • 2
    Implementation plan with milestones and timelines

    Built backwards from the target date. Created a structured plan in Monday.com with milestones, dependencies, stakeholder responsibilities, and communication checkpoints. Organised stakeholder time, tracked actions, and kept all teams aligned throughout.

  • 3
    Phased cohort rollout

    Divided rollout by client type to manage risk and adoption — strategic direct accounts first, then insurance clients. Each cohort had its own communication plan, consent process, and activation checklist.

  • 4
    Blocker identified and resolved mid-delivery

    During the first cohort, a Development → Training dependency hadn't been surfaced early enough. Training materials couldn't be completed because technical setup was still pending. I escalated, restructured the sequencing, and made technical completion a gate for training prep in all subsequent phases. Following cohorts ran significantly smoother.

  • 5
    UAT — wrote the test cases myself

    Designed and executed UAT test cases for the AI tool. Defined what "working" looked like, documented pass/fail criteria, and coordinated sign-off with the development team. Product was signed off when my delivery criteria were met.

💡
Key learning applied: In multi-team implementations, the biggest risk isn't technical — it's the assumption that everyone else has done their part. I now build dependency verification and cross-team alignment calls into every phase before the next one opens.
